📌 WHY IN NEWS
The uptake of induction stoves has been sluggish in low-income Indian households, with only a small minority of households using induction cooktops.

📚 GS3 — Economy: Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy

Only 5% of Indian households use induction cooktops, despite their energy efficiency and safety benefits. The high upfront cost and lack of access to electricity are major barriers to adoption.

✅ KEY FACTS
• Experts — Induction Cooktops: Only 5% of Indian households have electric penetration in their kitchens.
• Indian Government — Electric Cooking Methods: Promoting the use of electric cooking methods to reduce dependence on fossil fuels.
• Indian Households — Induction Stoves: Slow uptake of induction stoves due to high upfront cost and lack of access to electricity.
