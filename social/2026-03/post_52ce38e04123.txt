📌 WHY IN NEWS
The uptake of induction stoves has been sluggish in low-income Indian households, with only a small minority of households using induction cooktops.

📚 GS3 — Economy: Energy Efficiency and Conservation

Only 5% of Indian households use induction cooktops, despite their energy efficiency and safety benefits. The Indian government is promoting electric cooking methods, but more needs to be done to encourage adoption in low-income households.

✅ KEY FACTS
• Experts — Induction Cooktops: Only 5% of Indian households have electric penetration in their kitchens.
• Indian Government — National Mission on Enhanced Energy Efficiency: Promoting the use of energy-efficient appliances, including induction cooktops.
• Policymakers — Electric Cooking Methods: Concerned about the slow uptake of induction stoves in low-income households.
