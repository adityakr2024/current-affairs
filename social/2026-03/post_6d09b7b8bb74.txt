📌 WHY IN NEWS
The ASEAN Ministers are holding meetings to address the West Asia crisis prompted by the recent conflict in the Middle East, which has led to surging oil prices and disruptions to shipping and trade flows.

📚 GS2: International Relations + West Asia + Multilateral

The ASEAN Ministers are meeting to discuss the impacts of the West Asia crisis on the region’s economy, with a focus on surging oil prices and trade disruptions. The meetings are expected to result in a coordinated response to the crisis, with possible measures including emergency oil reserves and trade diversification strategies.

✅ KEY FACTS
• ASEAN — Emergency Meeting: Discussion on surging oil prices and trade disruptions with 10 member states.
• ASEAN — Trade Flow: 40% of trade passes through the Strait of Malacca, a critical shipping chokepoint.
• ASEAN — Oil Imports: 80% of oil supply comes from the Middle East, with 70% of the region’s oil supply imported.
