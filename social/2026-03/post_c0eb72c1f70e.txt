📌 WHY IN NEWS
The Supreme Court has ruled that parental income alone cannot decide OBC creamy layer status, in a case involving children of senior PSU officials selected as OBC candidates in civil service exams.

📚 GS2 — Polity: Social Justice and Empowerment

The Supreme Court has ruled that parental income alone cannot decide OBC creamy layer status. This decision will impact the selection process for civil service exams and the implementation of OBC reservations. The court's ruling clarifies the criteria for determining the creamy layer status of OBC candidates.

✅ KEY FACTS
• Supreme Court — OBC Creamy Layer Exclusion: Parental income alone cannot decide creamy layer status.
• Supreme Court — Civil Service Exams: Children of senior PSU officials selected as OBC candidates are not automatically excluded from reservations.
• Government of India — OBC Reservations: The creamy layer exclusion framework should not consider income from salaries and farm land for the wealth test.
