📌 WHY IN NEWS
The Supreme Court has ruled that parental income alone cannot decide OBC creamy layer status, in a decision that affects children of senior PSU officials selected as OBC candidates in civil service exams.

📚 GS2 — Social Justice: Other Backward Classes and Reservation Policy

The Supreme Court has ruled that parental income alone cannot decide OBC creamy layer status. This decision is expected to impact many OBC candidates who were earlier excluded from reservation benefits due to their parental income. The Court's ruling is based on the idea that the creamy layer concept is meant to exclude those who are already affluent and do not need reservation benefits.

✅ KEY FACTS
• Supreme Court — OBC Creamy Layer: Parental income alone cannot decide creamy layer status.
• Government — Creamy Layer Exclusion Framework: Income from salaries, farm land cannot be included for wealth test.
• Supreme Court — Civil Service Exams: Children of senior PSU officials selected as OBC candidates are eligible for reservation benefits.
