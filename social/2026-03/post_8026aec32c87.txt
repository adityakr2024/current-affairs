The Indian rupee has depreciated to 92.3 against the US dollar due to a surge in global oil prices. The RBI is likely to intervene in the currency market to slow the pace of depreciation. Foreign Institutional Investors have sold stocks worth ₹58,676 crore, further accentuating the situation.

✅ KEY FACTS
• RBI — Intervention: The RBI is likely to intervene in the currency market to slow the pace of rupee depreciation, with a potential sale of dollars to avert a record low.
• Foreign Institutional Investors — Stock Sales: Foreign Institutional Investors have sold stocks worth ₹58,676 crore as of March 13, further accentuating the situation.
• Crude Oil Prices — Surge: The surge in global oil prices has increased India's import costs and risked widening the current account deficit.
