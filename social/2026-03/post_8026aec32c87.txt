📚 GS3 — Economy: Foreign Exchange and Currency Management

The Indian Rupee has depreciated to 92.3 against the US dollar due to a spike in crude oil prices, prompting Foreign Institutional Investors to sell stocks worth ₹58,676 crore. The RBI is closely monitoring the situation to ensure stability in the foreign exchange market.

✅ KEY FACTS
• RBI — Foreign Exchange Management: Monitoring the situation to ensure stability in the foreign exchange market.
• Foreign Institutional Investors — Stock Sales: Sold stocks worth ₹58,676 crore as of March 13, 2026.
• Government of India — Crude Oil Imports: High dependence on oil imports makes the country vulnerable to fluctuations in global oil prices.
