ISRO, AIIMS sign MoU for cooperation in space medicine and research

इसरो और एम्स ने अंतरिक्ष चिकित्सा और अनुसंधान में सहयोग के लिए समझौता ज्ञापन पर हस्ताक्षर किए

Context

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to promote joint research in space medicine and research. The MoU aims to advance human health, performance, and safety during human space missions. ISRO has stated that the joint research will be aligned with its priority areas. The space agency is working towards improving the health and safety of astronauts during space missions.

Background

ISRO has been working on human space missions, with the aim of launching its first manned mission, Gaganyaan, by 2023. The space agency has been collaborating with various organizations to advance its research and development in space technology. The MoU with AIIMS is a significant step towards achieving its goals in space medicine and research.

Key Points
  • ISRO — Space Medicine Research: Joint research with AIIMS to advance human health and safety during space missions.
  • ISRO — Gaganyaan Mission: Aims to launch its first manned mission by 2023.
  • AIIMS — Space Medicine Research: Collaboration with ISRO to promote research in space medicine.
  • ISRO — Priority Areas: Joint research will be aligned with ISRO's priority areas.
  • ISRO — Human Space Missions: Aims to improve the health and safety of astronauts during space missions.
Implication

The MoU between ISRO and AIIMS is expected to have a significant impact on the development of space medicine and research in India. The collaboration is likely to lead to advancements in human health, performance, and safety during space missions, which will be crucial for India's future space endeavors.

📌 The Supreme Court has ruled that parental income alone cannot decide the OBC creamy layer status, in a decision that favours children of senior PSU officials selected as OBC candidates in civil service exams.

Parental income alone cannot decide OBC creamy layer status: Supreme Court

माता-पिता की आय अकेले ओबीसी क्रीमी लेयर दर्जा तय नहीं कर सकती: सुप्रीम कोर्ट

Context

The Supreme Court has ruled in favour of children of senior PSU officials who were selected as OBC candidates in civil service exams. The court stated that the creamy layer exclusion framework cannot include income from salaries and farm land for the wealth test. This decision is significant as it clarifies the criteria for determining the creamy layer status of OBC candidates. The court's ruling is based on the idea that the creamy layer concept is meant to exclude those who are already privileged and have a higher socio-economic status. The decision is expected to impact the selection process for civil service exams and the eligibility of OBC candidates.

Background

The concept of the creamy layer was introduced to exclude those who are already privileged and have a higher socio-economic status from the benefits of reservation. The creamy layer exclusion framework was established to ensure that the benefits of reservation reach the most deserving candidates. The Supreme Court's decision is based on the precedent set by earlier judgments, including the 1993 judgment in the Indra Sawhney case, which established the creamy layer concept.

Key Points
  • Supreme Court — OBC Creamy Layer Exclusion Framework: Parental income alone cannot decide creamy layer status.
  • Supreme Court — Civil Service Exams: Children of senior PSU officials selected as OBC candidates are eligible for reservation benefits.
  • Government of India — Creamy Layer Exclusion Framework: Income from salaries and farm land cannot be included for the wealth test.
  • Supreme Court — Indra Sawhney Case: The 1993 judgment established the creamy layer concept to exclude privileged candidates from reservation benefits.
  • Department of Personnel and Training — Civil Service Exams: The selection process will be impacted by the Supreme Court's decision on the creamy layer status of OBC candidates.
Implication

The Supreme Court's decision is expected to have a significant impact on the selection process for civil service exams and the eligibility of OBC candidates. The decision clarifies the criteria for determining the creamy layer status of OBC candidates and ensures that the benefits of reservation reach the most deserving candidates. However, the decision may also lead to further litigation and debate on the creamy layer concept and its implementation.

India’s LPG production up 25% since supply maintenance orders; officials also urge refraining from panic booking

भारत का एलपीजी उत्पादन 25% बढ़ा; अधिकारी पैनिक बुकिंग से बचने का आग्रह करते हैं

Context

India's LPG production has seen a significant increase of 25% since the supply maintenance orders were issued. The country imports 60% of its LPG requirements, with 90% of it being routed through the Strait of Hormuz. This increase in production is expected to reduce the country's dependence on imports. The government has also urged consumers to refrain from panic booking, which can lead to unnecessary shortages. The LPG production increase is a result of the government's efforts to maintain a stable supply chain.

Background

The Strait of Hormuz is a critical shipping lane for India's LPG imports, and any disruptions to this route can have significant impacts on the country's energy security. In recent years, the government has taken steps to diversify its energy sources and reduce its dependence on imports, including increasing domestic production and investing in alternative energy sources.

Key Points
  • The Government — LPG Production: Increased by 25% since supply maintenance orders.
  • The Ministry of Petroleum — LPG Imports: 60% of the country's requirements are imported.
  • The Ministry of Petroleum — LPG Routing: 90% of imports are routed through the Strait of Hormuz.
  • The Government — Supply Chain: Maintaining a stable supply chain is a top priority.
  • The Ministry of Petroleum — Panic Booking: Urging consumers to refrain from panic booking to avoid unnecessary shortages.
Implication

The increase in LPG production is expected to reduce India's dependence on imports and improve the country's energy security. However, the government must continue to monitor the situation and take steps to maintain a stable supply chain, including investing in alternative energy sources and diversifying its energy imports.

Context

The Supreme Court order has allowed sugar mills to sell their excess sugar stock, which was previously restricted. The sugar mill association had been seeking this relief for some time, citing the high costs of storing the excess sugar. The order is expected to benefit around 500 sugar mills across the country, with a total excess sugar stock of around 6 million tonnes. The sugar industry is a significant contributor to the Indian economy, with an annual turnover of around ₹1.5 lakh crore. The order is also expected to help farmers, who will now be able to sell their sugarcane to the mills at a better price.

Background

The sugar industry has been facing challenges in recent years, with excess sugar production leading to a glut in the market. The government had previously imposed restrictions on the sale of excess sugar to stabilize the market. However, the sugar mill association had argued that these restrictions were unfair and were causing significant losses to the industry. The Supreme Court order is seen as a major victory for the association, which had been fighting for this relief for several years.

Key Points
  • Supreme Court — Excess Sugar Sale: Allowed sugar mills to sell their excess sugar stock.
  • Sugar Mill Association — Relief: Welcomed the Supreme Court order, citing relief for the industry.
  • Indian Sugar Industry — Annual Turnover: ₹1.5 lakh crore.
  • Sugar Mills — Number: Around 500 mills to benefit from the order.
  • Excess Sugar Stock — Quantity: Around 6 million tonnes.
Implication

The Supreme Court order is expected to have a significant impact on the sugar industry, with the potential to stabilize the market and benefit both sugar mills and farmers. However, the order may also lead to an increase in sugar prices, which could have implications for consumers.

Reports of Iran allowing Indian ships through Strait of Hormuz ‘premature’: Centre

ईरान द्वारा भारतीय जहाजों को होरमुज़ जलडमरूमध्य से गुजरने की अनुमति देने की खबरें ‘अतिशयोक्तिपूर्ण’ : केंद्र

Context

The Indian government has been expressing concern about the targeting of merchant navy ships that are stuck both inside the Gulf as well as in the Gulf of Oman near the Strait of Hormuz. The Strait of Hormuz is a critical waterway for international trade, with over 20% of the world's oil passing through it. The Centre's statement comes amidst rising tensions in the region. India has been seeking assurances from Iran to ensure the safe passage of its ships. The issue has been discussed at various levels, including diplomatic channels.

Background

The Strait of Hormuz has been a point of contention in the past, with Iran and the US having differing views on the use of the waterway. The current situation has been exacerbated by the ongoing tensions between Iran and the US, which have led to a significant increase in security concerns in the region. The UN Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS) of 1982 provides for the right of innocent passage through the Strait of Hormuz.

Key Points
  • The Centre — Statement: Reports of Iran allowing Indian ships through the Strait of Hormuz are premature.
  • The Indian Government — Diplomatic Efforts: Seeking assurances from Iran to ensure the safe passage of Indian ships.
  • The Strait of Hormuz — Trade Importance: Over 20% of the world's oil passes through the Strait.
  • The US and Iran — Tensions: Ongoing tensions have led to a significant increase in security concerns in the region.
  • The UN — UNCLOS: The 1982 convention provides for the right of innocent passage through the Strait of Hormuz.
Implication

The development has significant implications for India's trade and economic interests, as the country relies heavily on the import of oil from the Gulf region. The Centre's efforts to secure the safe passage of Indian ships will be crucial in mitigating the impact of the ongoing tensions in the region.

संदर्भ

भारत सरकार ने खाड़ी और ओमान की खाड़ी में होरमुज़ जलडमरूमध्य के पास फंसे व्यापारी नौसेना के जहाजों पर हमले को लेकर चिंता व्यक्त की है। होरमुज़ जलडमरूमध्य अंतरराष्ट्रीय व्यापार के लिए एक महत्वपूर्ण जलमार्ग है, जिससे दुनिया के 20% तेल का परिवहन होता है। केंद्र का बयान क्षेत्र में बढ़ते तनाव के बीच आया है। भारत ईरान से अपने जहाजों की सुरक्षित यात्रा सुनिश्चित करने के लिए आश्वासन मांग रहा है। इस मुद्दे पर विभिन्न स्तरों पर,包括 राजनयिक चैनलों पर चर्चा हुई है।

मुख्य बिंदु
  • केंद्र — बयान: ईरान द्वारा भारतीय जहाजों को होरमुज़ जलडमरूमध्य से गुजरने की अनुमति देने की खबरें अतिशयोक्तिपूर्ण हैं।
  • भारत सरकार — राजनयिक प्रयास: ईरान से भारतीय जहाजों की सुरक्षित यात्रा सुनिश्चित करने के लिए आश्वासन मांग रहा है।
  • होरमुज़ जलडमरूमध्य — व्यापार महत्व: दुनिया के 20% तेल का परिवहन होरमुज़ जलडमरूमध्य से होता है।
  • अमेरिका और ईरान — तनाव: जारी तनाव के कारण क्षेत्र में सुरक्षा चिंताएं बढ़ गई हैं।
  • संयुक्त राष्ट्र — UNCLOS: 1982 के सम्मेलन में होरमुज़ जलडमरूमध्य से निर्दोष पassage के अधिकार का प्रावधान किया गया है।
महत्व

इस विकास का भारत के व्यापार और आर्थिक हितों पर महत्वपूर्ण प्रभाव पड़ेगा, क्योंकि देश खाड़ी क्षेत्र से तेल के आयात पर बहुत अधिक निर्भर है। केंद्र द्वारा भारतीय जहाजों की सुरक्षित यात्रा सुनिश्चित करने के प्रयास क्षेत्र में जारी तनाव के प्रभाव को कम करने में महत्वपूर्ण होंगे। भारत को अपने आर्थिक हितों की सुरक्षा के लिए ईरान और अन्य देशों के साथ राजनयिक प्रयास जारी रखने होंगे।
