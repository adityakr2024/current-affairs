सुप्रीम कोर्ट ने केंद्र के प्रस्ताव को मंजूरी दी, ट्रिब्यूनल सदस्यों की अवधि बढ़ाई

Context

The Supreme Court has given its nod to the Centre's proposal to extend the term of tribunal members. This move will affect those appointed under the Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021. According to Attorney General R Venkataramani, a new law is likely to be enacted by next September, either in the upcoming Budget session or the Monsoon session. Around 21 members are due to retire before the new law is enacted. The extension is intended to ensure continuity until the new legislation is in place.

Background

The Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021 was enacted to overhaul the tribunal system in India. The Act aimed to streamline the functioning of tribunals and ensure their independence and efficiency. However, the implementation of the Act has been slow, and the Centre has been working to address the issues arising from it.

Key Points

  • Tribunal Members — Retirement: Around 21 members due to retire before new law is enacted.
  • Centre — Legislation: Aims to ensure continuity and address issues arising from Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021.
