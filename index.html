Union Cabinet approves international status for Madurai airport

केंद्रीय मंत्रिमंडल ने मदुरै हवाई अड्डे को अंतरराष्ट्रीय दर्जा दिया

Context

The Union Cabinet has approved the international status for Madurai airport, which is expected to boost the airport's connectivity with foreign countries. This move is likely to increase the number of regular international routes and make it easier for foreign airlines to operate from the airport. The airport currently handles domestic flights and some international charter flights. The new status is expected to increase the airport's revenue and create more job opportunities. The approval is also expected to promote tourism and trade in the region.

Background

The Madurai airport has been seeking international status for several years, and the approval is a significant milestone for the airport. The Airports Authority of India (AAI) has been working to upgrade the airport's infrastructure to meet international standards, including the construction of a new terminal building and the expansion of the runway.

Key Points
  • Union Cabinet — International Status: Approved for Madurai airport.
  • Airports Authority of India (AAI) — Infrastructure Upgrade: Constructing a new terminal building and expanding the runway.
  • Madurai Airport — International Routes: Expected to increase regular international routes.
  • Foreign Airlines — Approval: Easier approvals for foreign airlines to operate from the airport.
  • Airport Revenue — Increase: Expected to increase the airport's revenue and create more job opportunities.
Implication

📌 Why in News: The Kerala Cabinet has approved the formation of a new anti-narcotics bureau to tackle the growing drug menace in the state.

Kerala Cabinet approves new anti-narcotics bureau

केरल कैबिनेट ने नई एन्टी नार्कोटिक्स ब्यूरो को मंजूरी दी

Context

The Kerala Cabinet has given its nod to set up a new anti-narcotics bureau, which will be headed by an ADGP-rank officer. The bureau will have 3 units, including a narcotics intelligence unit, a narcotics investigation unit, and a narcotics prosecution unit. The state government has allocated ₹10 crore for the initial setup of the bureau. The move is aimed at curbing the increasing drug-related crimes in Kerala, which has seen a 25% rise in such cases over the past year.

Background

The decision to set up the anti-narcotics bureau comes in the wake of a significant rise in drug-related cases in Kerala, with the state witnessing a 25% increase in such cases over the past year. The Kerala Police had been pushing for a dedicated agency to tackle the issue, and the state government had announced its intention to set up the bureau in the 2022 state budget.

Key Points
  • Kerala Cabinet — Anti-Narcotics Bureau: Approved formation of a new bureau to tackle drug menace.
  • Kerala Government — Anti-Narcotics Bureau: Allocated ₹10 crore for initial setup.
  • Kerala Police — Anti-Narcotics Bureau: Will have 3 units, including narcotics intelligence, investigation, and prosecution units.
  • Kerala Government — Anti-Narcotics Bureau: Move aimed at curbing 25% rise in drug-related crimes.
  • Kerala Cabinet — Anti-Narcotics Bureau: Bureau to be headed by an ADGP-rank officer.

Now, Rajasthan Assembly passes Bill to repeal two-child norm for ULB elections

राजस्थान विधानसभा ने दो बच्चों की नियम को निरस्त करने वाला विधेयक पारित किया

Context

The Rajasthan Assembly has passed a Bill to repeal the two-child norm for Urban Local Body (ULB) elections. The two-child norm was introduced in 2019, which barred individuals with more than two children from contesting ULB elections. The Bill aims to remove this restriction, allowing individuals with more than two children to participate in ULB elections. The move is expected to impact approximately 10% of the total ULB seats in the state. The Rajasthan government has stated that the decision was made to promote inclusivity and increase participation in local governance.

Background

The two-child norm was introduced in 2019 as part of the Rajasthan Panchayati Raj (Amendment) Act, 2019. The Act aimed to promote family planning and population control, but it was met with criticism from various quarters, with many arguing that it was an unfair restriction on individuals' rights to participate in the democratic process.

Key Points
  • Rajasthan Assembly — Repeal Bill: Passed to remove two-child norm for ULB elections.
  • Rajasthan Government — ULB Elections: Approximately 10% of total seats expected to be impacted.
  • Rajasthan Panchayati Raj (Amendment) Act, 2019 — Two-Child Norm: Introduced in 2019 to promote family planning and population control.
  • Rajasthan Assembly — Repeal Bill: Aims to promote inclusivity and increase participation in local governance.
  • Rajasthan Government — ULB Elections: Decision made to remove restriction on individuals with more than two children.
