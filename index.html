The Supreme Court's order comes amidst 60 lakh pending cases in West Bengal. The Election Commission's handling of electoral rolls has been under scrutiny. Background

📌 Why in News: The Supreme Court has approved the Centre's proposal to extend the term of tribunal members appointed under the Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021.

Supreme Court approves Centre's proposal extending term of tribunal members

उच्चतम न्यायालय ने केंद्र के प्रस्ताव को मंजूरी दी, ट्रिब्यूनल सदस्यों का कार्यकाल बढ़ाया

Context

The Supreme Court has approved the Centre's proposal to extend the term of tribunal members appointed under the Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021. This decision will affect around 21 members who are due to retire by next September. The Attorney General, R Venkataramani, stated that a new law is likely to be enacted by the next Budget session or the Monsoon session. The Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021 will continue to be in effect until then. The extension of the term of tribunal members will provide continuity and stability to the tribunals.

Background

The Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021 was enacted to reform the tribunal system in India. The Act aimed to streamline the functioning of tribunals and make them more efficient. However, the implementation of the Act has been slow, and many tribunal members were due to retire, creating a vacuum in the system.

Key Points
  • Supreme Court — Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021: Approved the Centre's proposal to extend the term of tribunal members.
  • Attorney General — R Venkataramani: Stated that a new law is likely to be enacted by the next Budget session or the Monsoon session.
  • Tribunal Reforms Act of 2021: Will continue to be in effect until the new law is enacted.
  • Centre — Proposal: Extended the term of around 21 tribunal members who are due to retire by next September.
  • Supreme Court — Decision: Provided continuity and stability to the tribunals.
